# E-commerce-Operations-Sales-Dashboard-Google-Sheets
## Project Overview

This project focuses on transforming raw e-commerce transaction data into a comprehensive business intelligence solution for monitoring sales performance and operational efficiency. Using a dataset containing over 10,000 customer transactions, I developed an end-to-end analytics workflow in Google Sheets that supports data-driven decision-making for e-commerce businesses.

The project began with extensive data cleaning and standardization to address inconsistent formats, missing values, duplicate records, and data quality issues commonly found in operational exports. A dedicated validation framework was implemented to identify anomalies and ensure reporting accuracy before analysis.

After preparing the dataset, I built a series of pivot table reports to analyze key business areas including revenue performance, order volume, product category trends, sales channel effectiveness, payment outcomes, refund patterns, and fulfillment activities. These reports served as the foundation for an interactive dashboard designed for business owners, operations managers, and decision-makers.

The final dashboard consolidates critical KPIs, operational metrics, and trend analyses into a single reporting interface, enabling users to monitor business performance, identify operational bottlenecks, evaluate sales channels, track customer activity, and uncover opportunities for growth. The solution demonstrates how structured reporting and business intelligence practices can transform messy operational data into actionable insights.

## Business Problem

E-commerce businesses generate large volumes of transactional and operational data daily. However, much of this data exists in raw exports that are difficult to analyze and often contain inconsistencies, missing values, duplicate records, and formatting issues.

The objective of this project was to transform raw transaction data into a structured reporting solution that enables stakeholders to monitor performance, identify operational challenges, and make informed business decisions through data visualization and analytics.

---

## Dataset Overview

The dataset contains over **10,000 e-commerce transactions** and includes information related to:

- Orders
- Customers
- Products
- Product Categories
- Revenue
- Refund Amounts
- Payment Status
- Fulfillment Status
- Shipping Methods
- Sales Channels
- Transaction Dates

---

## Data Cleaning & Preparation

The raw dataset required significant preprocessing before analysis. Data cleaning activities included:

- Standardizing date formats
- Handling missing values
- Removing duplicate records
- Cleaning revenue and refund fields
- Standardizing payment and fulfillment statuses
- Validating sales channels and product categories
- Creating derived fields for reporting and trend analysis

The cleaning process ensured data consistency and improved the reliability of business reporting.

---

## Data Validation & Quality Checks

To ensure reporting accuracy, multiple validation checks were performed, including:

- Duplicate record detection
- Missing value identification
- Revenue validation
- Refund verification
- Fulfillment status validation
- Payment status verification
- Order completeness checks

These checks helped identify and resolve data quality issues before dashboard development.

---

## Analysis & Reporting

Pivot tables were developed to analyze:

- Monthly Revenue Trends
- Revenue by Product Category
- Revenue by Sales Channel
- Payment Status Distribution
- Refund Analysis
- Fulfillment Performance
- Operational Performance Metrics

These reports formed the analytical foundation for the dashboard.

- Revenue Trend Analysis
- Product Category Performance Monitoring
- Sales Channel Analysis
- Refund Tracking
- Payment Status Monitoring
- Fulfillment Performance Tracking

- Executive-Level Reporting Layout

---

## Key Insights

- Website generated the highest revenue across all sales channels.
- Home & Kitchen emerged as the top-performing product category.
- Paid transactions accounted for the majority of completed orders.
- Refund activity was concentrated within a small number of product categories.
- Fulfillment performance highlighted operational improvement opportunities.
- Revenue trends revealed seasonal fluctuations and sales growth patterns.
